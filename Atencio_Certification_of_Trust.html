Certification of Trust

Special Needs Trust for the Benefit of Alicia R. Atencio
Under the Pedro and Diane M. Atencio Trust, Dated September 7, 2016

As provided in § 46A-10-1013 NMSA 1978, this Certification of Trust is evidence of the Special Needs Trust for the benefit of (f/b/o) Alicia R. Atencio, established under the Pedro and Diane M. Atencio Trust dated September 7, 2016 (the “Trust”), as follows:

Special Needs Trust — Beneficiary Protection Notice

This trust is a Supplemental Needs Trust established to enhance the quality of life of the Beneficiary, Alicia R. Atencio, without displacing or supplanting any federal, state, or local public benefits to which she may be entitled, including without limitation Supplemental Security Income (SSI), Medicaid, and any housing assistance programs. All powers set forth herein are expressly subject to this limitation and may not be exercised in any manner that would render the Beneficiary ineligible for such benefits.

Trust Provisions — Certifications

  1. 1. On September 7, 2016, Pedro Atencio and Diane M. Atencio (the “Grantors” or “Settlors”) executed a revocable living trust under the laws of the State of New Mexico.
  2. 2. Pedro Atencio died on November 13, 2016. Diane M. Atencio died on October 19, 2025. Upon the death of the surviving Settlor, the trust became irrevocable by its terms and its assets are held solely for the benefit of the named beneficiaries.
  3. 3. Article XI of the Pedro and Diane M. Atencio Trust establishes testamentary trusts for each of the Grantors’ three children. Javier Atencio was initially appointed as Trustee of the Trust; however, he has declined to serve. Pursuant to Article III, Section 6 of the Atencio Trust, a majority of the beneficiaries of the Atencio Trust may appoint a successor trustee. In accordance with that authority,                              has been duly appointed as Trustee of the Special Needs Trust for the benefit of Alicia R. Atencio.
  4. 4. Property may be taken into the Trust by being titled as follows:
    “Testamentary Trust f/b/o Alicia Atencio under the Pedro and Diane M. Atencio Trust dated September 7, 2016,               , Trustee.”
    No property shall be held in the name of the Beneficiary individually as a result of any trust transaction, and no transfer shall be made from the Trust that would cause trust assets to be counted as the Beneficiary’s personal resources for purposes of any public benefit means test.
  5. 5. The Trust’s taxpayer identification number (EIN) is 41-6960554. The Trust is governed exclusively by the laws of the State of New Mexico, including the New Mexico Trust Code, N.M. Stat. Ann. § 46A-1-101 et seq.
  6. 6. Trustee Powers — Enumerated and Limited. The Trustee has the power, acting alone, to exercise all Trust powers as set forth in the Trust Instrument, including the following powers, each of which is subject to the overriding obligation set forth in Provision 9 below and the Beneficiary Protections set forth in Section III of this Certification:
    1. To hold, manage, and safeguard Trust assets;
    2. To access, administer, and manage digital assets of the Trust in accordance with N.M. Stat. Ann. § 45-8A-1 et seq. (Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act), limited strictly to assets belonging to the Trust;
    3. To vote stock held in the Trust;
    4. To buy, sell, manage, and invest Trust assets, including shares of investment companies and common funds, without duty to diversify and irrespective of the laws of investment, and at premium or discount prices;
    5. To maintain a securities brokerage account on margin solely in connection with Trust assets;
    6. To purchase and sell options solely in connection with Trust assets.
    These powers are exclusive and exhaustive. No powers beyond those expressly enumerated herein or in the Trust Instrument are granted to or may be exercised by the Trustee.
  7. 7. The Trust is irrevocable. No Trustee, beneficiary, or third party may revoke, rescind, terminate, modify, or amend the Trust or any of its provisions. Any purported revocation, modification, or amendment not authorized by the express terms of the Trust Instrument and applicable New Mexico law shall be void and of no legal effect.
  8. 8. The Trust has not been revoked, modified, or amended in any manner that would cause the representations contained in this Certification of Trust to be incorrect. This Certification of Trust supersedes any and all prior certifications of the Trust. Any person, institution, or entity relying on this Certification may do so without requiring production of the full Trust Instrument, as authorized by § 46A-10-1013 NMSA 1978.
  9. 9. The information herein is true and correct in all material respects. The undersigned certifies under penalty of perjury that the contents of this document accurately reflect the terms of the Trust Instrument as of the date hereof.

Trustee Limitations & Beneficiary Protections

Hardened Beneficiary Protections — Binding on All Successor Trustees

The following limitations are binding upon the Trustee and any successor trustee and shall be construed in favor of the Beneficiary, Alicia R. Atencio, at all times:

  1. A. Sole Benefit. All Trust assets shall be held, managed, and distributed solely for the benefit of Alicia R. Atencio during her lifetime. No assets shall inure to the benefit of any other person, including the Trustee, except as otherwise expressly required by law.
  2. B. Supplemental Distributions Only. Distributions from the Trust shall be made only for purposes that are supplemental to and not in replacement of any public benefits or government assistance for which the Beneficiary is eligible or receiving. The Trustee shall not make any distribution for goods or services that are the responsibility of a government benefit program without first obtaining written guidance from qualified special needs trust counsel that such distribution will not adversely affect the Beneficiary’s benefit eligibility.
  3. C. No Self-Dealing. The Trustee shall not engage in any self-dealing transaction, directly or indirectly, including purchasing Trust assets for personal use, lending Trust funds to the Trustee or any related party, or deriving personal financial benefit from Trust administration beyond reasonable compensation as authorized by the Trust Instrument and N.M. Stat. Ann. § 46A-7-708.
  4. D. No Commingling. Trust assets shall not be commingled with the personal assets of the Trustee or any other individual or entity. All Trust funds shall be held in clearly designated Trust accounts bearing the Trust name and EIN.
  5. E. Duty to Account. The Trustee shall provide Alicia R. Atencio, or her legal representative, with a written annual accounting of all Trust receipts, disbursements, investments, and assets, in accordance with N.M. Stat. Ann. § 46A-8-813. The Beneficiary shall have the right to inspect Trust records upon reasonable request.
  6. F. Spendthrift & Non-Alienation. The Beneficiary’s interest in the Trust is not subject to voluntary or involuntary transfer, assignment, pledge, or encumbrance. No creditor of the Beneficiary may attach, garnish, or otherwise reach Trust assets prior to distribution.
  7. G. No Implied or Residual Powers. The Trustee possesses only those powers expressly conferred by the Trust Instrument and enumerated in this Certification. No power shall be implied from general fiduciary status, and no residual or catch-all power is granted. In any case of ambiguity regarding the scope of Trustee authority, the ambiguity shall be resolved in favor of the Beneficiary’s protection and preservation of her public benefit eligibility.
  8. H. Medicaid & Public Benefits Compliance. The Trustee shall administer the Trust in a manner consistent with all applicable rules and regulations governing supplemental needs trusts, including the Medicaid State Plan requirements applicable to the State of New Mexico and all federal guidance issued under 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4). The Trustee shall notify the New Mexico Human Services Department (Medicaid) of any changes in the Trust as required by applicable law.
  9. I. Payback Provision. Upon the death of Alicia R. Atencio, the Trust shall reimburse the State of New Mexico, through its Medicaid agency, for the total amount of medical assistance paid on behalf of the Beneficiary, to the extent required by 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A), before distribution of any remaining assets to other remainder beneficiaries as specified in the Trust Instrument.

Nothing in this Certification of Trust shall be construed to expand, alter, or modify the terms of the Trust Instrument. This Certification is provided solely as an evidentiary document pursuant to § 46A-10-1013 NMSA 1978. Any third party receiving this Certification is on notice that the Trust is a Special Needs Trust subject to the limitations described herein.
