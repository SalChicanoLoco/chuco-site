Section 01

Executive Summary

Alicia R. Atencio is the beneficiary of the Alicia Atencio Trust, created under the Living Trust for Pedro Atencio and Diane M. Atencio, dated September 7, 2016. Cardinal Trust Inc. accepted appointment as Successor Trustee on March 9, 2026. On April 15, 2026, through its attorneys at Hurley Toevs Styles Hamblin & Panter, PA, Cardinal Trust gave written notice of its resignation as trustee, citing "recent communications from you, including threats of litigation."

Cardinal Trust's resignation creates an immediate vacancy in the trusteeship. Per Article Three, Section 6 of the Trust, if no separately designated successor trustee exists, the vacancy shall be filled either by a majority in interest of the beneficiaries eligible to receive distributions, or by a court of competent jurisdiction. Cardinal's resignation is effective upon appointment of a successor trustee.

Immediate concerns requiring attorney action

  • Essential bills (PNM, propane, loans) remain unpaid as of April 15, 2026
  • No successor trustee has been appointed — trust is in administrative limbo
  • Cardinal Trust will only act to preserve assets — no distributions until successor is named
  • Two law firms represent Cardinal Trust — beneficiary requires independent counsel urgently
