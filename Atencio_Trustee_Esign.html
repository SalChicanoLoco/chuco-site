II. Trust Identification

Special Needs Trust Notice This trust is established pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 1396p(d)(4)(A). Distributions must be made solely for the Beneficiary's supplemental needs and shall not be used to replace, reduce, or supplant public benefits including SSI, Medicaid, or other means-tested programs. The Trustee has a fiduciary obligation to administer this trust in a manner that preserves the Beneficiary's eligibility for such benefits.

V. Trustee Covenants and Duties

By accepting appointment, the Successor Trustee covenants and agrees to the following:

5.1 Fiduciary Duty. The Trustee shall act in the best interests of the Beneficiary, with the care, skill, prudence, and diligence that a person of ordinary intelligence and prudence would exercise under similar circumstances, in accordance with N.M. Stat. Ann. § 46A-8-804.

5.2 Special Needs Administration. The Trustee shall administer the trust solely to supplement the Beneficiary's receipt of public benefits, including but not limited to Supplemental Security Income (SSI) and Medicaid. The Trustee shall not make any distribution that would disqualify the Beneficiary from receiving public assistance benefits.

5.3 Prohibited Distributions. No distribution shall be made for items or services covered by government benefit programs unless a qualified special needs attorney has determined in writing that the distribution will not adversely affect the Beneficiary's benefit eligibility.

5.4 Recordkeeping. The Trustee shall maintain complete and accurate records of all trust assets, receipts, disbursements, and investments, and shall provide annual accountings to the Beneficiary.

5.5 Loyalty. The Trustee shall act in the sole interest of the Beneficiary and shall avoid any conflict of interest. The Trustee shall not profit personally from the administration of this trust beyond reasonable compensation as authorized by the Trust Instrument.

5.6 Compliance. The Trustee shall comply with all applicable federal and state laws governing special needs trusts, including applicable reporting requirements and any state Medicaid agency notice requirements.
